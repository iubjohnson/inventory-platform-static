# Reports overview

> **Available on Growth and up.**

Reports turn your data into answers — what's tied up in stock, what's selling, how your suppliers perform, and what's moved. They're organized into four groups.

`[SCREENSHOT: a report with the period picker]`

## Inventory & valuation

- **Inventory by location** — on hand, available, committed, and incoming per product per location, valued at cost.
- **Valuation** — total stock value rolled up by location, category, supplier, and health.
- **Dead / aged stock** — in-stock products that aren't selling, aged by time since last sale, with the cash tied up.
- **Overstock** — products above their suggested maximum, and the excess value.
- **Reorder worksheet** — a buy list grouped by supplier with suggested quantities and estimated cost.

## Sales & profitability

- **Monthly sales** — units and revenue by month.
- **ABC analysis** — products ranked by revenue share, classed A/B/C. (See [ABC classification](../concepts/abc-classification).)
- **Sell-through** — units sold vs. on hand, with sell-through %, velocity, and weeks of cover.
- **Top sellers** — best sellers by revenue (sort ascending to find your slowest movers).
- **Gross margin** — revenue, cost, and margin per product.

## Purchasing & suppliers

- **Spend by supplier** — value of goods received, by supplier.
- **Open POs** — orders awaiting receipt, with outstanding value and overdue flags.
- **Lead-time performance** — actual delivery time vs. the supplier's estimate.
- **Fill rate** — units ordered vs. received per supplier, with shortfalls.

## Operations & audit

- **Shrinkage** — cycle-count variance (units and value), by session and by product.
- **Transfers** — inter-location movements, shipped vs. received.
- **Movement ledger** — every stock movement (sale, receipt, transfer, adjustment, count) over the period.

## Common controls

Most reports have a **period** picker (last 30 / 90 / 365 days, or a custom range). Every report can be exported to **CSV** or **PDF**.

> **Note:** Gross margin is an approximation — it uses each product's current cost rather than the exact cost at the time of each sale. The Movement ledger shows a capped number of rows on screen; export to CSV for the complete list.
