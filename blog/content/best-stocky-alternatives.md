# Best Stocky alternatives for Shopify (2026), honestly compared

With Stocky shutting down on **August 31, 2026**, Shopify merchants need a new home for forecasting, purchase orders, and stock counts. The right alternative depends on your size and complexity: small and mid-size merchants who want reorder planning without ERP overhead are Stockwik's sweet spot (full disclosure: Stockwik is our product); larger operations with manufacturing, wholesale channels, or warehouse workflows may be better served by a heavier platform. Here's the honest lay of the land.

> **Note:** We build Stockwik, so read this the way you'd read any vendor's comparison — we've kept every claim factual and we'll tell you plainly where Stockwik is *not* the right fit. Verify current pricing and features on each app's own listing; they change.

## What Stocky actually did for you

Before comparing, list what you're replacing. Stocky covered: demand forecasting and reorder suggestions, purchase orders and receiving, supplier records, stocktakes, and inventory reports. Note that some of it was already gone — **multi-location transfers were removed from Stocky in 2025** — and it required a Shopify POS Pro subscription. Whatever you pick should cover the parts of that list you genuinely use, without forcing you into software built for a much bigger company.

Shopify's own admin has been absorbing basic pieces of Stocky's job — stock tracking by location, transfers, simple purchase orders, low-stock alerts. If all you used Stocky for was recording POs, the built-ins may be enough. What the admin doesn't do is the *planning*: it won't calculate when to reorder or how much to buy.

## How to choose: six questions

1. **Does it forecast, or just record?** The core of Stocky was telling you when and how much to order. Make sure the replacement computes real reorder points from your sales history — not just low-stock alerts at a level you set by hand. (How this should work: [How reorder levels are calculated](/docs/concepts/how-reorder-levels-are-calculated.html).)
2. **Does it cover the full buying loop?** Suggestion → purchase order → send to supplier → **receive against the PO**. Receiving is where many tools stop short, and it's where inventory accuracy is won or lost.
3. **Does it handle how your suppliers actually sell?** Case packs, MOQs, and pack-size rounding — or will you be converting units to cases in your head forever?
4. **Multi-location?** If you run more than one location, check both planning-per-location and transfers — the feature Stocky dropped in 2025.
5. **What does it cost, all-in?** Stocky was "free" but required POS Pro. Most replacements are standalone apps: compare their monthly price against what you're really paying today. ([Stockwik's pricing](/pricing.html) is public: plans from $29/mo with a 14-day trial.)
6. **Can you be running before August?** You want at least one full reorder cycle in parallel with Stocky. Tools that read your Shopify sales history directly — rather than needing a data migration — are running the same day you install.

## FAQ

### What is the best Stocky alternative?
There's no single answer — it depends on size and complexity. For small/mid-size Shopify merchants replacing Stocky's forecasting-and-purchasing job directly, Stockwik was built for exactly that. For manufacturing, warehouse ops, or apparel ERP, a heavier platform fits better.

### Do Stocky alternatives require POS Pro?
Generally no. Stocky required a Shopify POS Pro subscription; most replacements, Stockwik included, are standalone Shopify apps.

### Can I try an alternative while Stocky still works?
Yes, and you should — run both through at least one reorder cycle before August 31, 2026 and compare suggestions. See the full [migration checklist](/blog/stocky-shutting-down-migration-checklist.html).
