The mark

The Stockwik symbol is a replenish loop: a near-complete circle that breaks at the top, with four dots climbing toward the gap and a triangle poised to close it. It reads as a cycle of depletion and replenishment — stock running down, then topped back up — which is exactly what the product does. The centered dot anchors it and doubles as the "current stock level."

Color

Deep indigo carries the brand; amber is the accent that signals action and energy. The amber is deliberately used sparingly — for the depletion dots in the mark, and in the product UI for buttons, alerts, and highlights.

Typography

Rajdhani sets the wordmark, all headings, display text, and numbers — its condensed, squared, technical letterforms give the brand a fast, precise, efficient feel that matches the "stock + quick" idea behind the name. It's especially strong on figures, which suits a data-dense inventory tool. Inter handles body copy and small UI text (labels, table data, captions) for maximum legibility, since condensed faces get cramped at small sizes. Reserve Rajdhani for large display use; let Inter carry anything small. Both are free and open-source (SIL Open Font License) and load directly from Google Fonts.

Usage rules

  • Keep clear space around the mark equal to the diameter of the center dot.
  • Use the reversed (white) mark on indigo or dark photographic backgrounds.
  • Use the monochrome mark for engraving, faxes, single-color print, or watermarks.
  • Keep the wordmark lowercase — "stockwik", never "Stockwik" or "STOCKWIK" in the logo.
  • Don't recolor the mark outside the brand palette, or use amber for the arc.
  • Don't rotate the mark, change the gap position, or alter the number of dots (always four).
  • Don't stretch, skew, or add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines) to the logo.
  • Don't set the wordmark in any font other than Rajdhani.
